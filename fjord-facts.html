Fun Facts About the Norwegian Fjords

1. The word “fjord” comes from the Old Norse word *fjǫrðr*, meaning “where one travels across.”
2. Norway has over 1,000 fjords – more than any other country in the world.
3. The Geirangerfjord and Nærøyfjord are UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
4. Many fjords were carved by glaciers during the Ice Age, creating steep cliffs and deep waters.
5. Some fjords are so deep, cruise ships can sail within metres of the cliffs – it’s like sailing through a postcard.
6. The Sognefjord is the longest and deepest fjord in Norway, stretching over 200 km.
7. You can find waterfalls cascading directly into the fjords – including the famous Seven Sisters.
8. Despite being so far north, the fjords rarely freeze thanks to the warming effect of the Gulf Stream.
